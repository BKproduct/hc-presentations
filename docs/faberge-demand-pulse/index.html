Faberge Demand Pulse · Proposal 3 / 5  ·  Processing Logic

Processing logic, eight steps

Most platforms stop at counting. We carry every signal through eight stages so that what reaches your team is not data, but a decision input. The synthesis stage runs on Opus 4.7, the most capable reasoning model currently available, with an upgrade path built in for stronger successor models.

1
Parallel collection
Each source runs in its own worker every week. Raw output is stored verbatim, with full provenance, so any conclusion can be retraced to the original data point.
2
Normalisation and lexicon tagging
Every signal is mapped onto one schema and tagged against a curated jewellery lexicon (product, style, technique, stones, cuts, metals). This makes signals comparable across very different platforms.
3
Signal strength scoring
Each signal carries a score combining volume, intent purity (research vs comparison vs purchase), recency, and source trustworthiness. High volume with weak intent ranks below low volume with strong intent.
4
Theme aggregation
Individual signals collapse into themes by lexicon category. The top thirty themes per week represent the demand surface for that week.
5
Surge and decline detection
Each theme is compared against its own rolling baseline (8 to 26 weeks). Z-scores classify themes as surge, rising, stable, fading, or collapsing. The system learns what is normal for your category.
6
Buyer cohort tagging
Every theme is attributed to the most likely buyer cohort: UHNW collectors, aspirational affluent (HENRY), Asia UHNW, or Gen Z aesthetic. Every insight addresses a real buyer, not an average.
7
Synthesis on Opus 4.7
A reasoning model converts the week of evidence into editorial output: headline, scorecard, top signals, design briefs, sales triggers, marketing angles, risks, watch list. The synthesis layer upgrades automatically when stronger models release.
8
Render and deliver
Weekly PDF bulletin with consistent typography, plain-text summary for inbox skim, and a draft email lodged in your account for review before send. Human in the loop is preserved by design.

Why this is stronger than current pilots

  • Intent rather than mentions. Eight signal layers about the buyer, not three about the press wave around your peer set.
  • Aesthetic foresight, two to six months ahead. Embedding-based clustering of taste micro-shifts that platform tools do not run.
  • Asia signal, six to twelve months ahead. An early warning layer that mass platforms cover poorly — especially relevant for a heritage house with imperial codes.
  • Design brief layer. Each week hands the design team a concrete remit: what to create, for which segment, with what saturation window.
  • Owned by you. Built on Opus 4.7 today, scheduled to inherit successor reasoning models with no contract renegotiation.
  • AAS as steward. Connector maintenance, quarterly research, and adaptation to your team feedback are built into the monthly fee.
