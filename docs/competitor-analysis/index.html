Phase 3–4 — Pattern Analysis

5 patterns that win

Distilled from hook analysis, ad strategy, and growth mechanics across all 5 competitors.

01
Waitlist creates scarcity
Function collected 200K names. Superpower collected 150K. Zero paid acquisition needed — the waitlist IS the marketing. Creates anticipation and organic word-of-mouth.
02
Biological age as anchor metric
InsideTracker's "InnerAge" converts because it's concrete and personal. "You are biologically 43, not 50" is more compelling than "your markers improved." One number beats a dashboard.
03
Podcast owns the longevity audience
InsideTracker sponsors Huberman (18M), Peter Attia (2M). This exact audience = HealthCopilot's target. Cost-per-action is higher but conversion quality is unmatched.
04
Founder as credibility engine
Mark Hyman built Function Health's entire funnel on his 4M following. The founder IS the brand. For HealthCopilot: Boris at 50, real data, real insights = authentic authority.
05
SEO via competitor comparisons
OneTwenty captures intent at decision stage: "OneTwenty vs Function Health." High intent, low competition. Every HealthCopilot competitor is an SEO opportunity.
06
Hook formula (unlocked)
[Data you already have] → [incomplete answer it gives] → HealthCopilot → [complete answer]

Works because it doesn't sell a new product — it upgrades what they already own.
